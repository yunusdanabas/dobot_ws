# ME 403: Introduction to Robotics
**Technical Course Specification**

---

## 1. Course Overview
- **Course Code:** ME 403
- **Credits:** ECTS: 7
- **Prerequisites:** Systems and Control (ENS 206)
- **Corequisites:** Laboratory (ME 403L), Recitation (ME 403R)

---

## 2. Technical Description
This course covers fundamental theories and computational methodologies for designing and analyzing **autonomous robotic systems**.

**Key Subject Areas:**
- 3D rotations & rigid body motion modeling
- Forward and inverse kinematics (FK/IK)
- Jacobian relations & singularities
- Dynamic modeling of manipulators
- Path planning & object detection algorithms
- Motion/force tracking controllers
- Numerical simulation & Hardware-in-the-Loop (HIL) control

---

## 3. Objectives & Learning Outcomes
### Objectives
Analytical formulation of kinematic/dynamic equations, synthesis of trajectory/force controllers, and implementation of real-time HIL control on robotic hardware.

### Learning Outcomes
1. **Formulate** FK/IK problems for robot manipulators.
2. **Derive** dynamic models and implement kinematic/dynamic simulations.
3. **Perform** robotic perception tasks using sensors and utilize motion planning algorithms.
4. **Synthesize** motion and/or force tracking controllers.
5. **Utilize** numerical algorithms for robot simulation.
6. **Implement** real-time feedback control on physical robot platforms.
